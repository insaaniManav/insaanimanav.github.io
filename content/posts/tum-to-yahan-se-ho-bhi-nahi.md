A few months ago I found myself at a Zakir Khan show in the Prestige Centre for Performing Arts. He did a whole bit about Bangalore traffic, the Airport and other quirky things everyone notices the first few times they come to this town with perfect weather.
And then he ended the bit with a line that I still remember as the room was still erupting with laughter from his last punchline. He just said
> "*Tum kya has rhe ho, Tum to yahan se ho bhi nahi*". 

I still think of that line sometimes whenever I find myself back in a KIA bus heading to some place in HSR or Sarjapur my friends call their "New home".

Home is still Patna. Or Delhi. Or Ludhiana. New home is the 2BHK with three flatmates and the Blinkit guy who knows the order

I had never really understood the charm of Cubbon park. I hate running in general but this one time a friend took me there for a run, I only went because he promised me Dosa at the Airlines Hotel right after.
So at Cubbon - I saw so many runners show up with their Oakley shades and arm bands for phones . Everyone seemed to be enjoying their Sunday Morning. Everyone ran around for an hour in their Asics Gel Nimbus 25 (sorry, 26, or wait, are they on 27 now)
. Bitched about their bosses, work, flatmates and then rode back to the same flatmates on their Meteor 350s.

Or It's a Saturday night and they're at Toit. Reservations under the same guy's name. The same 350s parked outside or are they Himalayans this time(It's taller so you can just drive over traffic, that's the point of a Himalayan in the city btw).After finishing the tower of the absolutely delicious one of a kind Craft Beer and two plates of tiger prawns,
They all exclaim "We should do something different next week","Yes Let's go to goa",
"You know this friend of mine runs a supper club, she cooks amazing food and invites people over who pay to eat it at her place",
"Isn't that just a restaurant bro","No man its all homely, My girlfriend draggged me there last week She and the host were both Bengali na so..".

And then Bob's Bar next Saturday. Under the same guy's name.

It's a city of people who left.

And Monday at 8pm sharp, the whole group is on their phones. Naru reservations drop at 8. If they refresh at exactly the right second, maybe they get the chef's table. They want to watch him make the noodles. They want him to talk to them while he does it. They want, very specifically, a bowl of soup that comes with a person.

---

*What is home anyway I wonder as the same KIA takes me back to Terminal 2 to my 'Old home'*
